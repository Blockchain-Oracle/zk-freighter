## The three boundaries

Stellar's own documentation says it plainly: Stellar is a public blockchain — every transaction
is recorded on-chain and visible to anyone. Privacy exists inside specific tools you opt into.
ZK Freighter is built around that honesty:

| Step | Visibility | What an observer sees |
|---|---|---|
| **1 · Shield in** | ○ Public | Your address deposited X into the pool contract |
| **2 · Send privately** | ⛉ Shielded | A valid proof was accepted — no amount, balance, or counterparty |
| **3 · Unshield out** | ○ Public | An address withdrew Y from the pool |

Bridge arrivals (USDC landing from another chain via Circle CCTP) are also public — the wallet
says so in the flow.

## How the hiding actually works

Inside the pool, your money exists as **notes** — sealed commitments on-chain that prove a coin
exists without revealing its amount or owner. Spending publishes a **nullifier**, a one-time
"spent" stamp that prevents double-spends but cannot be traced back to the deposit. A
**zero-knowledge proof**, generated on your device, convinces the contract the transfer is valid
without revealing anything else. See [shielded transfers](/docs/how-it-works/shielded-transfers).

## Compliance without a custodian

- **Association set (ASP):** first-time shielding registers a membership leaf with an on-chain
  association-set contract, so the pool can include known-good funds and screen out flagged
  ones. Privacy and compliance coexist — this is not a mixer.
- **Selective disclosure:** you can produce a [read-only proof of a specific note](/docs/how-it-works/disclosure)
  for an auditor — no keys, no spend authority, nothing uploaded.

## What we refuse to claim

- **Not "anonymous", "fully private", or "untraceable."** Boundaries are public, and correlation
  is real: matching deposit and withdrawal **amounts**, close **timing**, and a small pool
  (**anonymity set**) can all weaken privacy. Sending common amounts and waiting after shielding
  improves it.
- **Today, pool transactions are signed by your own Stellar account.** An observer can link your
  account to pool activity at the envelope level (though never the amounts or counterparties of
  shielded transfers). A relayer that submits on your behalf is designed and planned — until it
  ships, we say this plainly instead of overclaiming.
- **Unaudited.** The shielded-pool engine (Nethermind's reference implementation) and this wallet
  are not security-audited. Testnet is the recommended surface; do not use real funds.
