Most wallet issues are local to your browser, not the network. This page covers the common
ones and the honest way to tell them apart.

## "The private engine stalled while reading shielded notes"

The private engine is the in-browser prover and note scanner. Its local scan cache lives in
**OPFS** (the browser's origin-private file system), and OPFS is **shared across every tab** on
the same site. A stale tab left open from an earlier session — or a heavy sequence of wallet
switches — can wedge the engine.

**Fix, in order:**

1. **Close every other wallet tab**, leaving just one. This alone resolves most stalls, because
   a second tab can hold the engine's storage open.
2. **Reset the private engine cache**: **Settings → Private engine → Reset private engine
   cache**. This wipes only the local OPFS scan cache and reloads. Your vault, seed phrase,
   public address, and activity are untouched.
3. Retry the shielded action.

The cache is also reset **automatically whenever you switch networks** (testnet ↔ mainnet),
because the scan cache is not network-specific and stale entries from one network must not leak
into the other.

## Sync is slow or shielded balance looks stale

Shielded balances are found by scanning every pool event since deployment. Two things make this
fast and reliable, and both can be checked:

- **The bootnode** serves the pool's warmed event history. Check its health — a healthy testnet
  bootnode reports `ready: true` with the indexer caught up (`lag: 0`). If your build points at
  a self-hosted bootnode that is down, the scan falls back to public RPC, which only retains a
  short window and can miss older notes.
- **Your local scan cache** — if it is corrupt, reset it as above.

If the bootnode is healthy (caught up, lag 0) and a reset does not help, the issue is worth
reporting rather than retrying.

## Is it me or the service?

A quick way to isolate a local problem from a real outage: open the hosted wallet in a **fresh**
browser tab. If the hosted wallet works but your local build does not, the difference is local
browser state (the cache above), not the backend.

## Network endpoints

The wallet works against public Stellar RPC alone; the hosted services make it faster and
friction-free. Their health is public:

- Testnet bootnode: `https://bootnode.zkfreighter.app/health`
- Mainnet bootnode: `https://mainnet-bootnode.zkfreighter.app/health`
- Funding API: `https://api.zkfreighter.app/health`

See [Services](/docs/architecture/services) for what each one does.
