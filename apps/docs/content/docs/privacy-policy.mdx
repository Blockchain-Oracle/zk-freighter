_Last updated: 2026-07-03._

ZK Freighter is a self-custody wallet — for the web, as a browser extension, and as a mobile
app. It is built so that we never see your money or your keys. This policy explains exactly what
that means.

## We do not collect personal data

- **No account, no sign-up, no email.** You are never asked to identify yourself.
- **No analytics, telemetry, tracking, ads, or third-party trackers** of any kind.
- We do not sell, rent, or share any data, because we do not collect any to begin with.

## Your keys and seed phrase never leave your device

Your seed phrase and private keys are generated on your device, encrypted with your password,
and stored **locally** (browser storage / the app's local storage). They are never transmitted
to us or anyone else. Zero-knowledge proofs are generated **on your device**; no server sees
your payment contents.

## What does leave your device

Using any wallet means broadcasting transactions to a public blockchain. Specifically:

- **Stellar network.** Transactions you make are submitted to public Stellar RPC/Horizon and are
  visible on-chain, exactly as with any Stellar wallet. Shield and unshield deposits are public
  boundaries by design — the wallet says so each time.
- **Optional hosted services**, which make the wallet faster but are not required:
  - **Bootnode** — serves the public event history of the privacy pool contracts. It receives
    ordinary read requests; it holds no keys and stores no personal data about you.
  - **Testnet faucet (funding API)** — if you request free testnet funds, it receives the
    destination address and your IP address **solely to rate-limit abuse**. This is testnet
    only and involves no real value.

You can point the wallet at your own services or public infrastructure instead; see the
[Install](/docs/install) and [Services](/docs/architecture/services) pages.

## Browser-extension permissions

The extension requests the minimum it needs:

- **`storage`** — to keep your encrypted vault and settings on your device.
- **`offscreen`** — to run the WebAssembly zero-knowledge prover off the popup so proving does
  not block the UI.

It does **not** request access to your browsing history, tabs, or the content of websites, and
it **fails closed** for external dapp key-access and signing requests — it is a privacy wallet,
not a general signer.

## Honest limits

ZK Freighter provides **shielded transfers**, not anonymity. Shielding in, unshielding out, and
bridge arrivals are public on-chain moments, and on-chain analysis (amounts, timing, pool size)
is always possible. We never claim the wallet is "anonymous," "untraceable," or "fully private."
It is early, unaudited software; use testnet.
