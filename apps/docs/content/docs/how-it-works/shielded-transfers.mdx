"Shielded" is Stellar's own word for a payment whose sensitive details are hidden with
cryptography. Here is the whole lifecycle, with the public boundaries marked:

## The moving parts

- **Commitment** — a sealed, tamper-proof receipt posted on-chain when money enters the pool.
  It proves a note exists without revealing its amount or owner: a locked box everyone can see
  but nobody can open.
- **Nullifier** — a unique one-time "spent" stamp published when you spend a note. It prevents
  double-spending but cannot be traced back to the original deposit.
- **Merkle tree** — all commitments are bundled into one short fingerprint, so a proof can show
  "my note is in the pool" without revealing which one.
- **The proof** — a Groth16 zero-knowledge proof over the BN254 curve, generated by a Rust→WASM
  prover **in your browser** (recorded at roughly six seconds on a laptop), then verified by the
  pool contract on-chain. Stellar's Protocol 25/26 upgrades added the native BN254 and Poseidon
  primitives that make on-chain verification affordable.

## First-time setup: shield access

Before your first shield, the wallet registers a membership leaf with the on-chain
**association-set (ASP)** contract. This is the compliance layer — the pool can include
known-good participants and exclude flagged funds. The wallet handles it in one continuous run:
setup transaction → wait for on-chain indexing → deposit, with live progress.

## Auto-shield

Auto-shield can move your public balance into your shielded balance for you, so you don't have
to shield by hand every time. It is off by default. See the dedicated
[Auto-shield](/docs/how-it-works/auto-shield) page for exactly how it behaves.

## Change stays private

Shielded transfers use a 2-in/2-out note design: when you spend, change comes back to you as a
**new hidden note**. There is no visible change output to correlate.
