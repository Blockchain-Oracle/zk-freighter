## Shielded pools: Circom / Groth16

The flagship path reuses Nethermind's `stellar-private-payments` engine (credited openly):

- **Circuit:** a 2-in/2-out "policy transaction" — every operation (deposit, transfer,
  withdrawal) is the same circuit with different public amounts. Change returns as a hidden
  note.
- **Prover:** Rust compiled to WebAssembly, running in the browser (in a worker on web/mobile,
  in an offscreen document in the extension). A proof takes ~6 seconds on a laptop.
- **Verifier:** the Soroban pool contract verifies the Groth16 proof on-chain over BN254 with
  Poseidon2 hashing — the primitives Stellar added natively in Protocols 25/26.
- **Binding:** the recipient and amount of a withdrawal are hashed into the proof
  (`ext_data_hash`), so nobody — not even a submitting service — can redirect funds.

## Confidential tokens: Noir / UltraHonk

The second mode (testnet-only preview) is original ZK Freighter work: Noir circuits
(`circuits/`) proved with Barretenberg's UltraHonk via `bb.js`, verified on-chain by a Soroban
verifier, with balances as homomorphic commitments on the Grumpkin curve
(`contracts/confidential-token`). Operations: register, deposit, merge, transfer, withdraw.

## Why proofs never leave your device

Both stacks generate proofs locally. A server-side prover would see your amounts and
counterparties — the exact thing the product exists to protect. The trade-off is honest: the
first load pulls multi-megabyte WASM artifacts, and proving costs a few seconds of CPU. The
wallet shows real progress for both.
