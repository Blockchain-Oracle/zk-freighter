title: Confidential mode
description: The second privacy mode — hidden amounts between public addresses. Testnet preview.
---

Confidential Tokens are the **complementary** Stellar privacy tool: they hide *amounts and
balances* while keeping sender and recipient addresses public — the inverse trade-off of the
shielded pool. Useful when the relationship is known (payroll, invoices between named parties)
but the numbers are nobody's business.

## Status: testnet-only, unaudited preview

This mode is original ZK Fighter work — Noir circuits proved with UltraHonk (`bb.js`) in the
browser, balances as homomorphic commitments on the Grumpkin curve, and a Soroban SEP-41
wrapper contract (built on OpenZeppelin's confidential-token preview). It is deliberately
scoped to **testnet** and clearly labeled in every surface.

## Operations

| Operation | What it does |
|---|---|
| Register | Creates your confidential account on the token |
| Deposit | Wraps public USDC into a hidden balance |
| Merge | Consolidates pending amounts into your spendable balance |
| Transfer | Sends a hidden amount to another registered address |
| Withdraw | Unwraps back to public USDC |

Every operation generates its proof on your device, like the shielded pool.

## Where to find it

The **Confidential** screen on web and extension, and the mobile Confidential flow. Amounts are
hidden; the participating addresses are public — the screen says exactly that.
