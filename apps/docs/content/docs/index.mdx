---
title: What is ZK Freighter?
description: A self-custody wallet for shielded XLM and USDC payments on Stellar.
---

ZK Freighter is a **privacy-by-default, self-custody wallet** for the Stellar network. Normally
every Stellar transaction is visible to anyone who looks. ZK Freighter lets you move **XLM and
USDC** so that the sensitive details — amounts, balances, counterparties — stay private, using
real cryptography rather than trust in a middleman.

The proofs are **zero-knowledge proofs**, generated **on your own device** in about six seconds,
and verified on-chain by Soroban smart contracts. No server ever sees your payment.

## The honest one-liner

> Public in. Private through. Your call out.

Stellar is a public blockchain — you cannot make everything disappear, and we never claim to.
What you get is **shielded transfers**: the link between money entering and leaving the pool is
broken, and payments *inside* the pool are private. Three moments are public, and the wallet
names them every time:

- **Shield in (deposit)** — visible on-chain
- **Unshield out (withdraw)** — visible on-chain
- **Bridge arrivals** — USDC arriving from another chain is visible on-chain

Everything between those boundaries is a shielded transfer.

## What it is not

- Not "anonymous", "fully private", or "untraceable" — see the [privacy model](/docs/privacy-model)
- Not a mixer — first-time shielding registers with an association-set (ASP) contract so the
  pool can screen out flagged funds
- Not a general-purpose public wallet — ZK Freighter builds the privacy layer only
- Not audited — this is early software; don't use real funds yet

## One wallet, three surfaces

The [web app](/docs/apps/web), [browser extension](/docs/apps/extension), and
[mobile app](/docs/apps/mobile) share one shielded pool and one core engine. Every feature is
consistent — only the shape changes.

## Compliance, on your terms

[Selective disclosure](/docs/how-it-works/disclosure) produces a **read-only proof of a specific
note** for an auditor or counterparty — without handing over keys or spend authority. You decide
what's seen.
